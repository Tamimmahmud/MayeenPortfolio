OGSLSC

A final project offered by the Deakin University, where we had to redesign website for a surfing club based in Ocean Grove, Victoria. As their website was outdated, so they wanted something that will attract more audience by it’s modern UI touch.

Lead Time: 3 Months
Sector: Lifesaving Club
Services: UX Design, UI Design
See Full Work
Objective

This report goes into depth of the redesigned website outlining a deep analysis of the current website and its flaws that impede Ocean Grove Surf Life Saving Club’s new business model with becoming a self-sufficient organisation. It is through an evaluation of the club’s website that determined their strategic position and how the revitalisation of the website could make this happen.

Privacy Statement

OGSLSC have recently updated the physical aspects of the club by having built their new clubhouse. Although they have been progressive in this respect, it's now their virtual aspects which now need to be updated. This of course refers to the current website of ocean grove which is outdated and unappealing for members or visitors of the website. To remain competitive, they must have a more contemporary website to ensure their virtual presence on the internet, matches their physical presence when people visit the club.

Project Scope

  • Create a website design that fits the club’s modern physical presence.

  • Promote the club’s events and give greater presence and generate more awareness.

User Research

Existing club members make up the largest proportion of the website users, and the majority of them are young adults aged 18-30. These individuals are interested in learning more about the club’s activities and renewing their memberships, therefore, our aim was to highlight the news and events section, as well as simplifying the membership renewal guide. The results of our research also indicated that people in this age bracket are sociable and would like to share stories in their life with their network via online platforms (Yellow 2018). Therefore, social media integration would be an aspect for us to consider while redesigning the website.

The second user type is new visitors who are interested in signing up for the membership. As they have little knowledge about Ocean Grove SLSC, it was important that information on the club values, membership benefits and surf lifesaving activities be highlighted and communicated in an engaging manner to encourage the visitors to join the club. Amongst these new visitors, there are also parents interested in signing their children up for the Nipper membership. As such, we would like to dedicate a part of the website to highlight information on training activities, safety and parent engagement.

Being the third user type, businesses and philanthropists are organisations and individuals interested in making sponsorships or donations to the club. These users would like to know if the club’s missions, purposes and goals align with their affinities, and how their financial support would make an impact. Our research also found that giving recognition to supporters helps retain and enhance engagement between the club and its advocates. Therefore, we planned to develop a stewardship section on the website to acknowledge the sponsors and donors.

Finally, event organizers visit the website to learn more about venue booking. The idea of FAQs was helpful for this type of users, as it could help the users to eliminate the time on booking the venue more effectively. They would also appreciate detailed information about the venue and would also like to have pictures of the function spaces – which are missing on the current website. Therefore, where it is appropriate, we planned to use less text descriptions and focus on conveying the information via images.

Logo

The logo was inspired by a Leaf. Here, Leaf represents client and the vein is Techforia, where they provide all the services so that client could grow. Similar to how veins transport water and nutrients throughout the plant.

Also, the leaf has T in it to convey the logic behind the logo.

Before finalizing my designs, I tested my high fidelity wireframes on several users to identify pain points, gather feedback and improve my designs.

Positives

  1. The design and the feel of the website fit very well with the Info-Tech design theme. I like the calmness of the design, and the colors were used beautifully.

  2. Really like the use of illustrations on the homepage, they suit the website.

  3. I find the logo beautiful and meaningful. And describes the team’s creativity and passion.

Paint Points

  1. I was not aware of the slider images, in both Home page and Team section.

  2. It’s hard to say when a section has ended and another one is starting.

Solutions

  1. Slider/ carousel icon is used below the images, therefore people wait to see the next image.

  2. A dark margin or divider has been used for a better understanding.
