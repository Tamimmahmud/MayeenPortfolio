Techforia

As a new company, Techforia was looking for an attractive and well decorative website. The company wanted to reflect their culture in the website as well - Positive.
Lead Time: 1 Month
Sector: Tech Support
Services: UX Design, UI Design
See Full Work

Objective

The project was to design a website for not-for-profit startup company. It is an info-tech company that aim to assist businenesses to accelerate their workflows with the help of modern technologies.

Problem

In a world of technology, there are lots of website that provide the same service as Techforia do.

Solution

“Techforia” puts emphasis on clients problem and provide software solutions based on the identified pain oints in a cheaper price than others.

User

The website is for people who’s looking for services including website design, business software solutions and search engine optimization.

Based on several user stories from the brief, a User Flow Diagram is created.

Story 1

As a new customer, I want to see all the information about the website in a single page.

Story 2

As a first time visitor, I want to see the services, projects and teams in the home page.

Story 3

As a new customer, I want to see the demo version of the projects that the company currently working on.

Story 4

As a new customer, I want to have the privilege to send message or email to the company from the website.

Style Guide: Color Palette

Motive behind choosing “Blue” and shades of blue is - For Tech company colors, blue is always true.
Blue is perennial choice in corporate branding - not only is it the color most people cite as their favourite, it’s also associated with power and success.

Logo

The logo was inspired by a Leaf. Here, Leaf represents client and the vein is Techforia, where they provide all the services so that client could grow. Similar to how veins transport water and nutrients throughout the plant.
Also, the leaf has T in it to convey the logic behind the logo.

Before finalizing my designs, I tested my high fidelity wireframes on several users to identify pain points, gather feedback and improve my designs.

Positives

  1. The design and the feel of the website fit very well with the Info-Tech design theme. I like the calmness of the design, and the colors were used beautifully.

  2. Really like the use of illustrations on the homepage, they suit the website.

  3. I find the logo beautiful and meaningful. And describes the team’s creativity and passion.

Paint Points

  1. I was not aware of the slider images, in both Home page and Team section.

  2. It’s hard to say when a section has ended and another one is starting.

Solutions

  1. Slider/ carousel icon is used below the images, therefore people wait to see the next image.

  2. A dark margin or divider has been used for a better understanding.
